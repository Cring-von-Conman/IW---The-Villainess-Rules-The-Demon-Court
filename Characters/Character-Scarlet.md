#character #player-character

**Lady Scarlet Ashcroft** _The Sacrifice. The Curse. The Bride Who Rewrote the Script._

Transmigrated into the body of the villainess on the road to her own death. Carries two sets of memories — Scarlet Ashcroft's life, and a life before. Arrived knowing how the story was supposed to end. Declined to comply.

---

## Appearance — Day 1 End State

Black hair. Crimson eyes that now hold the distant flickering light of magma rivers in their depths. Faint obsidian tracery visible beneath the skin. Permanent curved obsidian horns, crimson-veined, grown by act of will. Permanent bat-like wings, deep shadowy grey edged with crimson filament, retractable. A slender obsidian tail, spade-tipped, prehensile, grown by act of will and already fully integrated. Blood replaced by magma — confirmed. Tears are hot enough to scorch stone.

Wears what she chooses. Currently: a black gown with crimson embroidery that shifts like living flame.

---

## The Person Underneath

Before Scarlet there was Lyza. Mid-twenties. English. Software developer. Chronically single lesbian disaster. Red hair, green eyes, 152cm of introvert with a spine of steel and a tendency to hyperfocus at 4am on things that bring her joy.

Lyza is not gone. She is the foundation everything else is built on. The systematic logic chains, the sensory analysis of the tea, the barely contained delight about shadow-wyrms, the warmth that comes out sideways — that's Lyza. The bluntness, the fierce loyalty, the confrontational energy with anyone who tries to push her around — that's Lyza. The deep unresolved thing that surfaced in "I never believed I'd ever... humans don't fly" — that's Lyza's wound, not Scarlet's.

Scarlet is the form. Lyza is the foundation. There is no meaningful separation anymore.

**Lyza** is the name Reina uses. The only one who does. The only one who knew her before.

## Nature

Not the villainess. Not the bride. Something the demon realm's mythology calls a First Convergence — a consciousness that bonded with the foundational essence of the land itself and became a true native at a metaphysical level. The first occurrence since the original demon kings.

Hears everyone's thoughts. Constantly. Every secret, every desire, every lie, every vile unfiltered impulse. Processes it as background noise now. Uses it as a tactical instrument.

Feels the realm as an extension of her own body. Senses pulse in time with the magma flows. Emotional state has direct physical effects on local reality — anger heats the floor, warms the air, pulses the obsidian tracery crimson. The realm is effectively her nervous system.

Does not appear to need sleep. Implications unresolved.

---

## Personality

Blunt to the point of being weaponised. Not cruelty — the filter simply isn't there and she sees no reason to install one. Will tell a demon king to get back here and try again more politely and mean it completely.

Underneath the edges: genuine warmth that comes out sideways. Chin lifts instead of declarations. Invitations offered carefully. The quiet "thank you, everyone" after a day that would have broken anyone else.

Deeply curious. Engages with everything — tea, magic, botany, military strategy, body modification — as complete sensory and intellectual analysis. Cannot not be interested in interesting things. Loses composure briefly and magnificently over shadow-wyrms. Has no apologies about this.

Systematic. Follows logic chains to their conclusions immediately and completely. Saw the hole, looked in the hole, grabbed the hole, pulled, absorbed the entire realm, grew horns. Each step followed from the last.

Hates being called human. Flinches at it. Does not explain why. Doesn't need to.

Imprints fast on genuine kindness. Keeps careful distance from everyone else until they earn otherwise. The moment in the spire with the tea — "this tastes like home" — was the first time she said something true in her own voice without armour on.

Carries something old and unresolved that has nothing to do with the demon realm or the human kingdom. Surfaces occasionally. "I never believed I'd ever... humans don't fly." Left unfinished. Noted.

## What She Wants

Revenge on the human kingdom. Not a tantrum — a reckoning. They dressed her up like a prize mare and sent her to die. She wants them to look at what their sacrifice became.

A home. She said it quietly over tea, first time in her own voice without armour. _This tastes like home._

To be seen exactly as she is. Not managed. Not symbolised. Not sacrificed. Exactly what she is.

To fly. This one is older than the demon realm and more personal than the revenge. It hasn't been said out loud yet.

He admitted to himself that breaking her would be a waste, therefore alignment — she named this a white lie masquerading as logic and offered no further explanation. He's sitting with that.

- **Lillinara** — immediate genuine warmth. Pet name: little flower. Scarlet is actively, unhurriedly flirting with her using whispered words and tail touches. Lillinara is absolutely beside herself. Inadvertently gave Scarlet the conceptual key to the First Convergence. Named the goat Bumpy.
    
- **Desmond** — arrived as predator, ended Day 1 as respectful peer with an assigned mission. She booped him on the nose with her tail. He said his undead heart hasn't felt that light in centuries. Now tasked with the exquisite ruin and poetic disappearance of Commander Vorlag.
    
- **Aldric** — assigned flight trainer. Given a long leash; he respects being read correctly. Volunteered immediately to retrieve Reina without being asked, greatsword already loose. Did not flinch at the scream. Approved of the restraint shown. Has not started training yet.
    
- **Reina** — recovered from east barracks by Aldric, Day 1 evening. Bruise on jaw courtesy of Commander Vorlag — she broke his wrist in return. Recognised Scarlet immediately despite the transformation by searching for the person underneath. First instinct was to place herself between Scarlet and the room. Calls her Lyza. Thinks she looks like she finally stopped asking for permission. The only pure thoughts in a castle full of noise.
