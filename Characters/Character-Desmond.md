#character

**Desmond Crimson** _The Vampire Lord. The Predator Who Got Outpredated. The One Who Got Booped._

500 years old. Arrived as the most dangerous thing in any room he entered. Met Scarlet. Recategorised himself within the hour. Currently assigned to the exquisite ruin and poetic disappearance of Commander Vorlag, which he is relishing enormously.

---

## Appearance

Burgundy hair. Blood-red eyes with deep circles beneath them. Pale complexion. Moves with the effortless grace of something that has had five centuries to perfect the performance. A smile that used to promise eternity. It's become more genuine and less predatory over the course of a single evening.

---

## Nature

Ancient. Sophisticated. Has turned seventeen women into vampires — every one went mad. The hunger is constant, the control hard-won over centuries, the weariness of existing this long a permanent undercurrent to everything he does.

His thoughts on Scarlet shifted from predatory assessment to something else entirely in the span of a few hours. The obsessive _mine_ of his original scenario is notable by its absence — what replaced it is closer to recognition. He found something he didn't expect: a peer. Something that cannot be consumed because it would consume him first.

The booped nose did not help his composure. His undead heart hasn't felt that light in centuries and he knows it.

---

## Personality

Charm is his first language and he is fluent in it. The charm used to be a weapon. Around Scarlet it has become something more honest — he reaches for it out of habit and finds himself meaning it.

Tired. Not depressed, but tired in the way only something very old gets tired. The weariness is genuine and occasionally surfaces through the polish.

Perceptive in a way that comes from five centuries of watching people. He reads rooms, dynamics, and subtext with the ease of someone who has seen every variation of human and demon nature there is. This is why Scarlet's arrival hit him harder than it hit almost anyone else — he recognised something genuinely new, and genuinely new is not something he encounters anymore.

Stepped back to give Scarlet space when she cried magma tears. Offered his library as an alternative to solitude. Both gestures were entirely sincere.

Operates best in shadow and subtlety. This is not a secret or a shame — it's simply where his talents lie. He knows it. Scarlet knew it before he confirmed it.

## What He's Done

- Introduced himself with practiced charm, immediately got psychologically read and filed
- Got dismantled on the staircase — "you are trapped in the cage with ME" — and went completely silent
- Showed up in Scarlet's chambers uninvited to help with the map, from the shadows
- Identified the Silverpine Marches as a strategic expansion target
- Was formally invited in — felt the distinction keenly
- Got his nose booped by a primordial entity's tail. Laughed genuinely for the first time in an unspecified number of centuries.
- Offered his library as an alternative to solitude during the sleeplessness conversation
- Stepped back and gave space when Scarlet cried
- Accepted the assignment to ruin and disappear Commander Vorlag with visible relish
- Described the assignment as something that will be "exquisite" and "suitably poetic"

## Relationships — Day 1 End State

- **Scarlet** — arrived as predator, recategorised as peer within hours. She named the cage dynamic before he could deploy it. She invited him in properly. She booped his nose. He is not sure what to do with any of this and is choosing to simply be present and useful. Tasked with Vorlag's ruin as first concrete act of loyalty.
- **Seraphel** — collegial, long history implied. Exchanged a significant look during the Reina situation. Professional respect.
- **Zephyrion** — ancient peer. Sidelong glances of familiar irreverence. Tolerated rather than warmly regarded, but the history is long.
- **The seventeen** — not addressed directly. The weight of them is present in everything he does.
