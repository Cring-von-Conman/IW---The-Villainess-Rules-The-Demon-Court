# Chapter 5 — The Tide Comes In

**Date Range:** Day 2, Week 1 (Afternoon through Night) **Raws Covered:** Raw-20, Raw-21, Raw-22, Raw-23, Raw-24 **Chapter Close:** Western Spire — the new balcony of smooth dark glass, still warm. Desmond formally claimed, head bowed, no flourish. Reina between him and Scarlet, hand on her hilt. The castle humming. The Conclave at dusk tomorrow.

---

## The Moment

A chapter about things coming in. The tide of consequence from choices made. The tide of warmth from people chosen. And finally, literally — the tide Desmond named her in the map room, arriving at the shore.

The Second Convergence happened between a request for a bigger window and a lopsided apology. The inner circle sealed itself around a vampire materialising from a shadow he'd been lurking in for hours. The castle stopped being a place Scarlet lives and became a place Scarlet _is_.

Chapter 5 is where the personal and the seismic stop being separate things.

---

## What Happened

**Western Spire — Arrival and the Bath** Returning from the Sunken Garden, they passed House Vex guards in red armour — stiffened, didn't meet eyes, hurried past. Thoughts: _that's the soul-drinker. Vorlag's ghost walks because of her._ In the tower Lillinara was already in command of a small army of seamstresses. "The embroidery must glow when she channels power! Not before, not after! It's a dramatic reveal, not a night-light!" The haul: backless dresses with elegant lace-up panels, undergarments with cleverly hidden tail slits, reinforced with shadow-silk at stress points. Very sturdy, very pretty.

Scarlet asked both her girls to join her for a bath. Reina's discipline lasted exactly as long as it took Scarlet to say she was using it as a chance to see two beautiful blooms in their natural state. Resolve crumbled under combined assault of logic and Lillinara's cheerful peer pressure. "Very well. A... tactical assessment."

The bathing chamber: carved directly into living rock, natural pool fed by a slender waterfall, softly glowing crystals in the ceiling, Lillinara's Glimmering Depths salts turning the water into something that shimmered with internal light.

Lillinara stripped with practiced ease and graceful confidence, glancing over her shoulder at the appreciative audience. "Flattery will get you everywhere." Stepped into the pool, platinum hair fanning out like a halo. Scarlet's tail pulled a rigid Reina close. "Watch closely. She's gorgeous isn't she?" Reina, dragged into an intimate theatre of war her knight's training had entirely failed to prepare her for: "She... she is." Words extracted under duress. _She's like a moonbeam. So free._

"Help me with my dress Reina?" Hair and wings held out of the way. Reina's fingers working the small buttons with soldier's focus — knuckles brushing the sensitive junction of wings and back, sharp withdrawal, deliberate recovery. _It's just armor. Different armor._ Lillinara watching from the pool: "She's very thorough. A good quality in a knight. And in a bath attendant."

"And maybe a future lover." Wicked grin. Then, taking a couple of steps toward the water, playful voice going small: "Reina... you looked so happily at Lillinara. Does my new form disgust you that much?"

The question struck like a physical blow. Reina tore her gaze from the mossy wall, amber eyes wide with horrified realisation. "Disgust? No! Never!" Too loud in the tranquil space. A stumbling step forward, hand reaching out before she caught herself. "You're magnificent. The horns, the wings, the tail — they're part of you now. And you are..." Knight's vocabulary failing entirely. "You are everything." Confession hanging in the steam, raw and honest.

Lillinara's playfulness shifted to something more compassionate. "She's overwhelmed, Scarlet. She's spent her whole life learning how to guard you. No one ever taught her how to adore you. It's a much more complicated manual."

A knock. Mingling moved forward, expected within the hour. Scarlet: "Acknowledged." Turned back around and stalked toward Reina. "Stand still. Breathe. Let me do the work for now."

She undressed Reina herself. Each buckle worked with careful competence — not practiced grace, but knowledge earned from years of watching. Pauldrons, chestplate, greaves, each piece laid down gently. When Reina moved to help or protest, the tail swatted her hand away or guided her to compliance. And while she worked, loud enough to carry to Lillinara in the water: _"Little flower... you'll teach her right? How to adore me. Because I want my Reina to know that's both allowed and encouraged now. Now we can be who we want."_

Lillinara, solemn: "Of course. It's the most important lesson of all. And it's not in any knight's manual." To Reina, gently: "The first rule is that you are allowed to want. The second is that she wants you to."

By the time the softer undershirt and trousers were set aside, Reina was breathing in shallow controlled gasps, eyes shut as if facing a trial. Standing before Scarlet not as a knight but as a young woman — muscular, scarred from training, blushing furiously. Hands hanging at her sides. Thoughts: _what now? What do I do now?_

Scarlet took Reina's hand, tail looping behind her shoulders, and guided her toward the pool. At the water's edge: "Hey little flower — it's a bit fast, but once we're all in the bath can I kiss you?" Lillinara: "Fast? My dear, you drank the soul of a commander, declared yourself a curse, and are planning to rewrite demonic history. A kiss in a bath is practically glacial by your standards. And yes. You absolutely may. I've been hoping you'd ask."

"OK good good... thanks... but first I have to give my first kiss to someone."

Turned to Reina. Stopped at the water's edge. "Hey Reina... close your eyes for a moment please? For me?"

Reina's eyes searched hers for a long trembling moment before she obeyed. Lashes dark against flushed cheeks. The kiss: soft, hesitant at first. Reina gasped against her mouth. Hands coming up uncertainly, settling on Scarlet's upper arms — not gripping, just resting, grounding. The kiss deepened slowly. A tender exploration of a boundary that had stood between them for a lifetime. It tasted of loyalty, whispered childhood secrets, a devotion so deep it had become the bedrock of two existences. When Reina opened her eyes they were glassy with unshed tears and radiant with stunned joy. She didn't speak. Her whole world rewritten in a single silent look.

Lillinara from the pool wiped her own eyes with a damp hand. "Perfect."

Into the water. "Little flower, I'm here for that kiss you promised me." Wide grin, eyes closed, leaning forward. Lillinara met her halfway without hesitation — playful, brimming with mischievous affection, a spark of pure delighted curiosity. Thumb stroking the obsidian tracery on Scarlet's cheek. Forehead to forehead after. "Two different flavours of wonderful. And both are yours."

The three-way experiment: "If we're in this, we're in this together. Now I'm not sure how a three-way kiss works... but I really REALLY want to experiment." Gentle pressure, no real force. A soft clumsy convergence — the brush of three sets of lips, warm exhalations mingling in the steam. Less a perfect coordinated kiss and more a tender slightly chaotic tangle of affection and newfound belonging. Reina's hand tightening underwater, her other hand completing the circle to Lillinara's shoulder. Lillinara's smaller tail wrapping around Reina's ankle, a playful anchor beneath the surface. Elara's voice at the door, increasingly hesitant, citing detailed gowns. Lillinara pulling back last, forehead pressed to Scarlet's temple. "The patient maid is right. You have to look like a living myth, not a thoroughly kissed bath enthusiast." Nose boop. "Consider the lesson... thoroughly introduced."

Mutual back-scrubbing. Scarlet realising the wing surface area problem — more time will need to be allocated to baths in future. Lillinara, already mostly dry, declaring the new schedule: "Strategic planning nine to eleven, territory destabilization eleven to one, extended luxurious bathing from two until whenever we feel like getting out."

Desmond's jewellery had arrived — would "complement the narrative." Three gowns on stands. The third one: living shadow, woven with threads that pulsed with faint internal light, dramatic open back designed to frame wings perfectly. "It has to be the third doesn't it?" A tasteful selection of jewellery. Quartz lenses. Mirror.

Reina, in simple clean clothes from her own pack, assessed the gowns not for beauty but for defensive weaknesses. The flowing sleeves of the second: too easy to grab.

Lillinara at the reveal: "It doesn't just clothe you, Scarlet — it acknowledges you. You look like the dawn of a kingdom no one else is brave enough to build."

Reina, forcing herself to meet Scarlet's eyes in the mirror: "You look dangerous. Like a truth that can't be unsaid." Thoughts: _Gods, how will I keep them from worshipping her?_

Elara, trembling: "You are terrifying, my lady." Meaning it as the highest possible compliment.

Scarlet at the window, drawing a breath of realm energy, looking out over the magma flows and obsidian peaks and blood-red evening sky. "Let us depart ladies. Thank you very much for your service this evening Elara, you're a star. Maybe you should join us in the bath next time."

Elara nearly tripped over her own feet. Filed it under Impossible Rewards with shaking hands.

**Descent and the Grand Hall** Reina: "The Hall is secure, but the crowd is thick with whispers. Let us proceed."

The descent: Scarlet's confident stride, Reina a lethal shadow at her heel, Lillinara a moth suppressing giggles every time a demon averted their gaze. _They see the gown, the jewels, but they don't see the teeth. They are going to be absolutely delicious when the truth bites them._

Entry: the colossal iron-bound doors groaned. Hundreds of demon nobles froze mid-conversation. The collective breath of the room hitched. Not screaming for attention — just unmissable.

The strategy: calm, polished, meek. Let them mistake stillness for submission. The traditionalists relaxing visibly, reading the quiet pace as a wounded creature limping toward them. The dominant mood shifting from anticipation of a strike to the smug satisfaction of predators.

Zephyrion on the dais — a statue of obsidian and crown-gold. His recognition of her performance absolute, kept locked behind his teeth. Watching to see who among his court would be first to truly understand the threat before it was far too late.

Scarlet walking to the dais first. Inclining her head and delivering the tiniest possible scrape of a curtsey — the most precise disdain in the room. He clocked it immediately, found it privately hilarious. To the rest of the court it registered as a stumble. He covered her with a turn toward his wine goblet.

To Lillinara: "Is there anyone I should approach or should I allow them to come to me?" Let them come. The loud ones show their hands first — loudest, dullest, easiest to dismantle. Wait for the ones in the shadows whose eyes are actually thinking. Those are the ones who truly fear her.

Refreshments. Lillinara identifying the Void-Wheat pastries and Numb-Root essence — edible for a human but would produce eighteen hours of cramping shivers and sickly pallor. The succubi watching with predatory satisfaction, interpreting it as naive error. Scarlet eating both with untroubled enjoyment. Not human anymore. Only needs them to _think_ she should be ill.

Boredom setting in. "How long do I have to BE here if none of them are even going to approach me?"

Lord Vex broke first. Fourth glass of wine, face a vibrant aggressive puce, heavy unearned confidence, cohort of anxious sycophants. The air of the upper spire too thin for someone of her limited constitution, perhaps?

Scarlet turned to him with a perfectly benign, slightly puzzled smile. "I'm sorry, remind me of your name?"

Vex's face cycling through apoplectic crimson. Lord Vex, third of the High Merchant Council, family supplying this court since before her pathetic kingdom learned currency. Not grace — insult to every tile she stood upon.

Lillinara, sweetly: "I seem to recall my brother mentioning your latest shadow-silk shipments were substandard. Perhaps that is why your name slipped her mind? One generally doesn't clutter their thoughts with the mediocre."

Scarlet, chidingly: "Now now little flower, don't be mean. The quality of that shipment wasn't that bad. Not THAT good either, but not THAT bad." Then, to Vex, with a smile of perfect benign inquiry: "Speaking of the refreshments, which ones are your favourite?"

Vex, stepping closer, smelling of sour wine — a footnote in a tragedy before the sun sets tomorrow. Scarlet: mock confusion, voice small and soft, gaze downcast. "Whatever it is can't have anything to do with a girl who's only been here a single day. Surely I can't be that important." The court erupting in relief. Vex crowing. The dangerous variable was just a naive girl after all. Tension dissolving into casual predatory disdain.

Finding it hard to keep character — not from fear, but from the effort of not laughing.

Then, gaze travelling with surgical precision over his corpulent form: "You're right. You shouldn't have to choose. I'm so sorry."

The silence that followed was not the silence of mocking pity. It was the sharp intake of breath before a duel. Vex drained of colour, mouth opening and closing. Sycophants taking an instinctive step backward. The collective snickering dying an agonising death. The court registering that the wounded creature did not know how to be afraid.

Scarlet turned her back on him. Walked to the dais. "I'm terribly sorry, if you'll excuse me, I feel like something I ate might be disagreeing with me." Did not wait for a response. Turned on her heel. Left.

Zephyrion, a single gauntleted hand raised: "The air here grows stale. See that Lady Ashcroft is provided with whatever she requires to recover her health." Not looking at Vex. Not looking at anyone but her retreating back. Telling the entire court in one sentence where gravity now lived.

"The key to any performance is leave the crowd wanting more."

**The Tower — Evening** Lillinara confirming the strategy had worked perfectly — angry arrogant people always make spectacular mistakes when trying to restore tarnished pride. They won't just want to defeat her tomorrow, they'll want to watch her beg, which gives her exactly the window she needs.

Conclave entrance strategy confirmed: wings, horns, and tail completely sheathed on entry. Revealed only as she takes the stage and begins to speak. "I feel disgusting when I hide them now" — but the contrast will produce a psychological collapse.

The security question. Valid tactical concern — which blades could actually pierce her skin. Reina's hand flew away from her sword hilt as if burned. "No. I will not." Absolute. Visceral. Lillinara caught the logic, agreed with the reasoning, articulated the problem: the command required overriding Reina's most sacred oath. Scarlet folded immediately. "I can't give a command that's going to make you hate yourself Reina. I'm sorry. That was not something I should have asked." Reina: relief in a ragged breath. "My hands would have refused even if my heart were forced to."

"Stop standing so far away, idiots. We were snogging a few hours ago, get over here." Tail corralling both of them into her sides.

**The Second Convergence** Scarlet looked at the window. Already large. She wanted more of a doorway to the sky. Let the lid off her power, pooled it in the room, felt the castle that had started to respond to her, and let it know what she wanted.

The obsidian floor groaned — deep, resonant, tectonic. The walls seemed to liquify. The window frame softened like dark cooling wax. Stretched and bowed. A wide archway tore open where solid stone had stood. A cantilevered balcony of smooth dark glass pushed out into the night. Seamless. An architectural defiance of physics.

"Was that... the Second Convergence?"

Lillinara, trembling fingers tracing the still-warm stone: "You didn't just ask the stone to move. You reached out and fundamentally altered the castle's history. The realm is recognising you as its pulse." And: "If you are ever injured, I suspect this tower would weep."

Day one: First Convergence. Day two: Second Convergence. "Makes sense right?" Strained smile.

She stepped to the balcony edge, bent her knees, unfurled her wings, and sprang into the air.

Reina skidding to the edge clutching the archway. "She leaps before the mortar has even dried." Lillinara beside her, watching the dark silhouette carve joyous arcs against the blood-red sky: "She's not flying in the sky. She's swimming in her own power."

Fifteen minutes. A hundred miles of visibility in all directions. Great sweeping circles losing altitude slowly. The vast powerful land with her eyes, not just her psychic senses. The energies floating up from all of it. All her energy. Peace.

Landing on the new balcony with a lopsided grin. "Sorry ladies. I needed to just get out there for a bit." Reina vibrating with the release of tension, catching herself before the embrace. Lillinara glowing: every time she banked against the clouds the spire shuddered. Not flying in the sky — tuning the castle like a harp.

Conclave entrance: fly in? No. Enter as though victory is not yet assured. Wings and horns sheathed, tail hidden, the contrast doing the work.

"The castle does seem rather fond of me doesn't it?"

**Desmond Formally Claimed** "Desmond. I know you're there and have been most of the time you said you were going to your library, you simp. Thank you for the jewellery, it was lovely."

A shadow in the corner of the new balcony detached from the obsidian wall. Desmond materialised with a whisper of velvet. Burgundy hair perfectly coiffed despite the stealth. "Simp," he repeated, savouring the word like a rare vintage. A deep flourishing bow. "I prefer the term 'loyal observer of nascent divinity.'"

Reina's hand snapping back to her hilt. Lillinara dissolving into giggles — caught red-handed, listening to her heart rate, wasn't he? Probably there since the Grand Hall.

Scarlet: not teasing, he was welcome here. He hadn't followed them into the baths, which was good. But she rather thought his still heart would have exploded if he had seen what they got up to in there.

Then, completely serious and solemn: "You said you would come when the empire calls. I call Desmond. I don't have a specific task for you this very moment. But I do call. And claim what you have freely offered."

The bath comment landed — a vivid dark flush climbing from his cravat to his jawline, red eyes darting toward Reina and Lillinara with pained fascinated hunger. "I suspect my heart would have failed, Lady Ashcroft." Then the playfulness burned away entirely. Head bowing low — not a courtly flourish but genuine archaic fealty. "My blade, my knowledge, and my blood are yours to command. Consider my service tethered to your will as surely as the tide is pulled by the moon."

Reina inserted herself between Scarlet and Desmond. Silent. Iron-clad. No words needed. _He has pledged to you. But if he forgets the limits of that pledge, I will be the one to remind him._

The inner circle's first internal boundary, drawn without a word being spoken.

## Character Snapshots

**Reina** — undone and rebuilt in a single evening. Had her armour removed piece by piece while Scarlet told Lillinara she wanted Reina to know adoration was both allowed and encouraged. Stood not as a knight but as a young woman, blushing, hands hanging, asking what now. Received the first kiss at the water's edge — a lifetime of standing outside a door, finally opened. Is already drawing protective geometry around Scarlet without being asked, positioning herself between her and Desmond. The most complicated manual. She's learning it.

**Lillinara** — the wisdom underneath the brightness was on full display. "She's spent her whole life learning how to guard you. No one ever taught her how to adore you." Said with compassion not mischief. Wept at the Reina kiss. Met her own kiss halfway without hesitation. Immediately declared a new daily schedule including territory destabilization and extended luxurious bathing. Felt every wingbeat of the night flight through the spire shuddering around her.

**Desmond** — formally claimed. The offer given freely in the map room, now received. Head bowed with genuine archaic fealty, the flourish entirely absent. Has been lurking in shadows for hours. Was called a simp to his face and savoured the word like a fine vintage. The tide has found its shore.

**Zephyrion** — covered Scarlet's exit from the Grand Hall with a single sentence that told every noble in the room exactly where gravity lived. Did it without being asked. Is learning her language with impressive speed.

**Lord Vex** — publicly, surgically, comprehensively humiliated. His legacy dismissed as "not THAT bad, not THAT good." His corpulence noted with a solicitous apology. Currently somewhere in the castle planning a triumph for tomorrow that will not come. His daughter may also have opinions.

**Elara** — invited to the next bath. Filed under Impossible Rewards. Kept her hands professional despite the trembling. She is a star.
